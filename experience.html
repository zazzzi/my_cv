Experience

My early working life began with a lot of jobs in the restaurant business. I come from Italian heritage and a family that, when I was younger, ran a Italian bistro, so it felt natural for me to start my working life in that way.

In my 20s, I realized that I wanted to do something else. I didn't really know what, so I did what every young person does, try everything. I started studying 3D Animation at Högskolan Väst in Trollhättan but soon came to the conclusion that the graphical part of animation wasn't for me. Feeling a bit lost I took a job at a technical support center, at a company called Webbhelp Nordic. We handled technical support over the phone for a big American company, working mainly with laptops, tablets and phones.

After being there for less then a year, I got offered a job as a production assistant in Stockholm and moved to there. We created educational video material for lawyers, with focus on digital evidence management. This was a really great experience, but after about 7 months, when my part of the project was done, it was hard to find a new job and living arrangements in Stockholm. So I was forced to move back to Gothenburg.

The feeling of being in the same place as you were 2 years ago is always hard, so while picking up smaller jobs in media production I wanted to try something new. I got a job in retail, working in one of Whyreds storefronts in Gothenburg. Loving the social interaction that working in retail gave me, whilst working with clothing and fashion, I really enjoyed working at Whyred. The hours weren't great thought, so I applied to work at a store in Gothenburg called Vallgatan 12. A family owned business retailer that sells fashion and interior design. The time I spent there has been great and I still work part time during my studies.

So when does the development part of my life start? Over year ago I reconnected with an old friend, and in turn my old friend group. Most of them are either fullstack developers or studying to become one. My friend, who's currently is studying Systemutveckling (system development) soon after that moved in with me and my interest for coding grew a lot. Found out about Medieinstitutet and their Front End Developer program, applied and got accepted. I now feel like I finally found my calling in life and who knows that the future has to offer. Hopefully it includes a lot of development, wether it be Front End or Back End!
